Research Focus

Learning systems that connect perception, place, and action.

My work sits at the boundary between spatial perception and embodied decision making. I study how agents localize, navigate, and act using learned visual representations, whether the setting is satellite-ground matching, driving in simulation, or coordinated mobile robotics.

Research Directions

01

Cross-view localization

Matching ground-level trajectories to overhead imagery while handling viewpoint gaps, satellite coverage quality, and site-level ambiguity.

02

Autonomous driving

Training driving policies in CARLA with imitation learning and reinforcement learning, while paying attention to stability and transfer.

03

Robotics systems

Building physical AI systems that move from software experiments toward real robot behavior and multi-robot coordination.

04

Trajectory-aware reasoning

Using paths, temporal consistency, and motion patterns as evidence for localization, planning, and scene understanding.

Work

Main Projects

Cross-view geo-localization

CVGL Challenge

A WRIVA / VisymScenes cross-view geo-localization project focused on matching ground trajectories to satellite imagery through dense retrieval, quality-aware satellite audits, temporal reranking, and learned offset refinement.

Private research repo

Autonomous driving

CARLA driving agents

End-to-end driving experiments in CARLA, including a DAVE-2 style imitation learning pipeline and DQN-based autonomous driving agents that emphasize system stability, replay buffers, target networks, and simulator control loops.

DAVE2Net CARLA CARLA DQN

Robotics

Hoplite robot formation

A robotics systems project for moving AgileX Limo robots in formation, connecting perception, coordination, and physical deployment concerns in a real-world multi-robot setting.
